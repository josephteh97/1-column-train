  overwritten (both column_detect.pt and runs/.../best.pt are 19 MB May 6, no
  backup).

  Most likely root causes of the worse result:

  1. CORE_CORNER_PROB = 0.55 is way too aggressive. Over half of training tiles
  now contain a thick‑wall core. That's a heavy distribution shift away from the
   data that produced your good baseline — closer to a "feature replacement"
  than an additive augmentation.
  2. Corner offset can bury columns inside the wall slab. At line 899‑900,
  offsets [-size//3, 0, +size//3] for both x and y mean ~2/9 of corner columns
  get drawn fully embedded in the dark gray wall (offset +,+ at sx1/sy1; offset
  -,- at sx2/sy2; etc.). The model is being labeled to find columns where the
  bbox is mostly wall, which is a noisy signal.
  3. BATCH=2 with mosaic at imgsz=1280 gives unstable BatchNorm statistics.
  yolo11s is sensitive to this; a noisy gradient signal from tiny batches could
  easily knock 5–10 mAP points off.

  Recommended path forward (safe, minimum churn):

  - Keep the additive variant but dial it way down: CORE_CORNER_PROB = 0.12.
  - Constrain offsets so columns always lean outward from the wall, never buried
   inward. Pick the sign of the offset based on which corner.
  - Bump batch back up. If 8 GB VRAM still OOMs at imgsz=1280, batch=4, the
  right knob is mosaic=0.5 (halves peak memory) rather than batch=2.
  - Backup column_detect.pt before retraining so we don't overwrite a model
  again with no fallback.
